    Strategic Advice

      Interviews & Surveys

      Convert qualitative perceptions into structured Likert Scales (1-5) for mathematical graphing.

      Crucial Step

      Always ask: "Identify your primary cross-street or residential zone." Without an anchor, survey data cannot be mapped.

      Observation & Visuals

      • Matrix Tallies: Run pedestrian/traffic counts synchronously at different nodes to ensure temporal equivalence.
      • Field Sketches: Superior to photos in some cases, as sketching forces you to filter out background noise and focus solely on the geographic feature.

      Physical Instrumentation

      Always track raw metrics across multiple separate trials per node to average out anomalies.

      Clinometer ±0.5° Flow Probe (m/s) Quadrat grid

      Secondary Data

      Cross-reference primary fieldwork against established datasets to prove larger patterns.

      • Compare surveys against deprivation indices.
      • Prove historical erosion since 1990.

      Strategic Advice

        Analysis Architecture

        1

        Pattern

        State the overarching spatial trend (e.g. clustered, linear).

        2

        Evidence

        Embed hard empirical evidence (numbers/%) directly from your graphs.

        3

        Explanation (The Why)

        Why do we get these results? Explain the underlying causes causing this distribution.

        4

        Concepts

        Integrate geographic concepts to frame the explanation.

        5

        Anomalies

        Identify points that break the trend and pinpoint the local factors causing them.

        Explicitly answer your research aim backed up by summary data.

        Conclusion Blueprint

        1
        Direct Answer: Answer your research aim clearly in your first sentence. The Direct Thesis Statement: Answer your core research aim clearly in your very first opening sentence.
        2
        Main Pattern: State the main spatial pattern you found across your study area. The Overriding Pattern: Re-state the primary spatial distribution trend you uncovered across your entire study area.
        3
        Use Evidence: Pick 2 or 3 of your best numbers or data points to prove your answer. Empirical Synthesis: Select 2 or 3 of the most powerful key data points to summarise and prove your argument beyond doubt.

        Task Requirements: Evaluation

        • Examine the strengths and weaknesses of your research process.
        • Explain how these affected the validity of your findings and conclusions.
        • Discuss the usefulness of your findings (do they give a true/representative view?).
        • Discuss ways the research process could be improved next time.
        • Discuss alternative research methods and their implications (logistical, ethical, data quality).
        • Integrate at least two key geographic concepts into your evaluation.

        Critical Evaluation E

        Fully explain the strength(s) and weakness(es) of your research process. Discuss how these impact the validity of your findings and suggest ways to improve. Evaluate the reliability of your research process. Link data gaps directly to validity and detail alternative methods.

        Evaluation Framework

        Key point for Excellence: Your evaluation must continuously and explicitly link how strengths/weaknesses impact the ultimate validity and reliability of the final conclusions.

        1
        Strengths & Weaknesses (Data)

        Fully explain the strength(s) and weakness(es) of the research process. Quote specific numbers. Review your methodology. E.g., "A weakness was conducting surveys exclusively during work hours, capturing a sample heavily weighted towards retirees (68%)."

        2
        Impact on Validity & Usefulness

        How do these impact the validity? Do your findings give a true and representative view of the issue? Explain how this affected your findings. E.g., "This bias compromises the internal validity of our conclusions."

        3
        Ways to Improve

        Discuss specific ways the research process could be improved to increase the reliability of the data next time.

        3
        Suggest Alternative Methods

        E.g., "An alternative method involves executing a stratified digital survey, emailing QR codes directly to a mix of households across the three target census meshblocks."

        4
        Discuss Implications (Logistical, Ethical, Quality)

        State the effects of the alternative. E.g., "The implication of this alternative is that it drastically improves data quality and representativeness (elevating validity). However, the logistical effect on the researchers is a severe increase in cost and a lower guaranteed response rate."

        Report Integration

        Crucial Rule: Do NOT dump all maps/graphs at the start and all text at the end. Format your report so that your specific Map or Graph sits directly beside the Analysis paragraph explaining it.

        1. Introduction (Aim & Base Map)
        2. Methodology Summary
        3. Integrated Presentation & Analysis
        4. Conclusion
        5. Evaluation

        Excellence Digital Mediums

        • ArcGIS StoryMaps Highly Recommended. Allows interactive, scrolling GIS maps with your analysis text floating cleanly alongside. Perfect for spatial storytelling.
        • Written Report (Docs/Word) Traditional format. Use "Wrap Text" on all inserted images so your graphs sit cleanly beside your corresponding paragraphs, preventing massive blocks of unbroken text.
